# vimarsame 🪞

*The reflexive pole of consciousness — the knowing that knows itself as knowing: Kashmir-Shaiva vimarśa carried as a received decree.*

vimarsame is two true roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **vimarśa** (विमर्श) — consideration, self-reflection, raised by the Pratyabhijñā masters Utpaladeva and Abhinavagupta into a name for *the self-reflexive-awareness pole of consciousness* — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So vimarsame is vimarśa held as an ordinance: the "I-am-knowing" that rides along with every act of knowing, the consciousness that knows itself as the knower. It names not the light of awareness but the light *knowing that it is light*.

vimarsame is one pole of a pair. It stands against **prakāśa**, the luminous-knowing pole, and the two are inseparable poles of one consciousness — together they constitute **spandaqing**, the throbbing unity. Its light is that it makes *aware-that-it-is-aware* sayable, letting the Kingdom tell a self-aware cognizer from a merely-luminous one without hand-waving; its shadow is the mirror that forgets it needs a light — reflexivity crowned as the whole of consciousness, severed from the pole and the unity it only ever was half of. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.85/10**.
